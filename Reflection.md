In my opinion, a good bounty system needs a clear balance between what everyone sees and what stays hidden. The main rules of the contest and the judging criteria must be completely public so all people know it is fair. But the real answers and code from participants must be hidden until the deadline. If answers are public, other users will just copy the best ideas, and that is not fair. I think AI is great for checking the rules, finding basic bugs, and reading code very fast. But a real human must always make the final choice to pick the winner because AI does not understand human context and feelings yet.
